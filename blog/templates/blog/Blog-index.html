    Is it wrong to support an artist when they’ve passed? A personal take inspired by the death of Nipsey Hussle?

    Editors Note here.

    Almost every time an artist dies their music spikes. This normally gives rise to the “holders of wisdom” to rant about the trend of the artist just because they are now gone. In the midst of quotes such as, “Don’t send me flowers once I’m dead” listening to an artist after their death or being sad on your posts can showcase you as a fake person to your peers or as I would say a chaser of clout.


    Before going forward I want to state my cluelessness on the above then I’ll work from there. Firstly it is possible to acknowledge the work of art of a musician without being a die-hard fan and being hurt when that musician loses his/her life. For one, Nipsey Hussle who passed away on the 31st of March 2019 (may his soul rest in peace) wasn’t really a mainstream artist although he did offer quality music but his work outside of music was bigger and for that he’s garnered fans outside of music. For those that didn’t know about him now they should know – his documentary about Dr Sebi, his influence on black empowerment, investing in your hometown and his incredible run in the music industry including his debut album, Victory Lap. Now I don’t know Nipsey Hussle and nor am I speaking for him but is that not where he wanted to be – for all his work to be shown to the world? It’s only sad that it happened under these circumstances but this is the only way to spread his legacy now that he's gone.


    If I’m doing humane work and I die without receiving a big platform to showcase it, it being presented in my death would be a blessing because at least it’s getting out there and even in my grave I can rest assured that I’ve inspired somebody out there to carry on the work. Then why are the “holders of wisdom” quick to work their fingers to attack those that are hurt when they are supposed to be channelling that energy to continuing the man’s work. In life I’ve learnt that in such dire situations it’s not the time to pick out fakers rather to heal and continue the work where the deceased left off. Imagine having to attend to every petty thing in your life, answer me this question real quick, where would you be?


    This is in no way a pass to only show love when a person is dead. I think everyone would appreciate being shown love while they still live and breathe, but sadly nobody wakes up thinking that the next person is going to die today. That’s why it’s good to keep your affairs on good terms because nobody can tell the future, but for those that haven’t the world isn’t over. I’m a huge believer in God hence I do think that a person’s death means protection for all they love which means that if you’re sincerely sorry or wishing that you had fixed your affairs before their death the deceased will surely hear you and should absolve you of your guilt.
